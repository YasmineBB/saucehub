Smokey and Earthy

Ancho

This is a lovely mild, earthy sauce that can also be used as a flavourful marinade.

You will need:

  • 4 Dried Ancho Chillies
  • 1/2 Red Onion
  • 2 Cloves Garlic
  • 100ml White Wine Vinegar
  • 175ml of Water
  • 1/2tsp Salt
  • ½ tsp Oregano

Instructions:

  1. Toast the chillies in a pan on medium heat for 1 minute
  2. Soak the chillies in hot water for 30 minutes
  3. Chop up the onion and garlic and blend alongside the rest of the ingredients until a smooth consistency is formed
  4. Leave to chill and store in the fridge for approx 2 weeks.

Simple but Impactful

Cayenne

A nice vinegary hot sauce with an impactful but not overpowering level of heat to liven up any dish.

You will need:

  • 200g Fresh Cayenne Chilli Peppers
  • 150ml Distilled Vinegar (apple cider/white wine work best)
  • 100ml Water
  • ½tsp Salt

Instructions:

  1. Bring all of the ingredients to a boil until the peppers have softened
  2. Blend the mixture until smooth, add a splash more vinegar or water if you prefer a thinner consistency
  3. Strain the mixture and store for approx 2 weeks in the fridge

Fiery but Fresh

Scotch Bonnet

A fiery but fresh dose of heat to bring a taste of the tropics to any dish!

You will need:

  • 4 Scotch Bonnet peppers
  • 1/2 White Onion
  • 1/2 Mango
  • Juice of 2 Limes
  • 1tsp Salt
  • 175ml water

Instructions:

  1. Puree the peppers, onion and mango together to a smooth consistency
  2. Pour the mixture in a pan, add the water and simmer for 20 mins
  3. Add the lime juice and salt and simmer for another 10 mins, adding a splash of water if you prefer a thinner consistency
